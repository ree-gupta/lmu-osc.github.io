Research Data Management Plans

A Research Data Management (RDM) plan documents how you will acquire, store, use, and share your research data. Draft it early - much of the content will be needed for ethics approval. Your DMP is a living document that evolves with your project.

Collecting new data? Write your RDM plan before submitting your ethics proposal. The plan helps structure your consent forms and data handling procedures.

Using existing data? Review the dataset’s metadata and codebook first. Check any data use agreements and understand how the data was collected.

What to include in your DMP:

Data Description
  • Types and file formats
  • Expected volume and growth
  • Naming conventions
  • Versioning approach
Storage & Security
  • Storage locations
  • 3-2-1 backup rule
  • Access controls
  • Encryption requirements
Sharing & Preservation
  • Repository selection
  • License choices
  • Embargo periods
  • 10-year preservation (LMU)
Ethics & Privacy
  • Consent procedures
  • Anonymization methods
  • GDPR compliance
  • Data use agreements
